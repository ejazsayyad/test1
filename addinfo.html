Additional Information

  Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer related deaths in the world accounting for 18% of cancer deaths. The overall last 5 years survival rate with lung cancer is 14%. Nonetheless, patients with early stage disease who undergo curative resection have a 5-year survival rate of 40% to 70%. The most recent statistics according to the American Cancer Society indicates that in 2014, there were 224,210 new cases, accounting for about 13% of all cancer diagnoses in USA.

  Large amount of research work has been carried out in the past towards CAD system for lung cancer detection using CT images. Lung cancer detection using thoracic CT scan images could potentially save hundreds of thousands of lives every year. Analyzing hundreds of thousands of CT Scans are an enormous burden for radiologists and may cause them to suffer from observer fatigue. Deep learning systems may help alleviate these problems and provide faster and more efficient detection techniques. For this purpose, we are interested in deep learning techniques like Convolution Neural Networks for the detection of lung nodules in CT scan images.
